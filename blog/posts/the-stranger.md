# Side project meets personal hobby

I've been attending a book club organized here in Taipei once a month. This book club is called [Read Around the World](https://meetu.ps/e/PXqBc/LCysF/i) and we select a book that is manageable to be read within a month and gather in a coffee shop to discuss about the book. So far I've attended three events and today I will be attending my fourth one. This month we have been reading Albert Camus' book "The Stranger."
<img width="542" height="962" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3132fc42-d6dc-4a75-9f4e-3f0e0f5e154a" />
But at the same time I was building a book reading app that is focused on Ethiopian literature. I was trying to focus solely on Ethiopian market but after attending the book club a couple of times I was thinking why not make this app focused on book clubs as well. Therefore, the last month I pivoted and made it my mission to customize the app to book clubs. So I added features for creating book clubs, book reading sessions and posting discussion questions.

# Why build yet another app?

Well I have been building apps for quite some time now and I have more than enough experience as far as technicalities are concerned. But I have never really pushed so much on my own ideas but rather I have always been working for someone. It's not like I haven't tried before but not putting enough effort on it in the past. But recently, I have started asking myself why I'm not putting enough effort on my own ideas. Honestly, I have reached a point in my life where working for someone is a little tiring. Therefore, this is my first real attempt to try to make something of my own.

# My approach to build the app

I have worked on an early stage startup before (working for someone). Unfortunately, that early stage startup didn't really took off and in the end I got burnt out and decided to quit. This is a while back (I might write something about it) and I have learned things and would like to believe have gained more wisdom. Therefore, I'm doing things slightly differently than before. In the previous startup I mentioned we failed not because we lacked technical skills or ideas. I believe we failed because of our vision and execution. We tried to be too perfectionist and focused on adding more and more ideas. But this time, I'm building features that I only believe would matter. I'm also moving much faster from idea to implementation. I was literally building the new feature to post discussion questions in the morning before the meetup. I'm not trying to make the app feature packed from the start but rather focus on the things that really matter.

# Tech details

I've chosen to use Ruby on Rails (RoR), which is literally the OG of web frameworks. It allows me to move faster from idea to implementation and it has everything a web app needs. I'm also self-hosting the app on my Mac Mini at home. I'm not going to be spending money on a VPS or other hosting service for an idea that might not even work. Thanks to Cloud Flare there is no noticeable difference in terms of speed as far as I'm concerned and I don't have that much traffic. It will continue this way and I actually want things to go naturally and slowly.
