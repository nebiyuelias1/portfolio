I've been attending a book club organized here in Taipei once a month. This book club is called [Read Around the World](https://meetu.ps/e/PXqBc/LCysF/i) and we select a book that is manageable to be read within a month and gather in a coffee shop to discuss the book. So far I've attended three events and today I will be attending my fourth one. This month we have been reading Albert Camus' book "The Stranger."

But at the same time I was building a book reviewing app that is focused on Ethiopian literature. I was trying to focus solely on the Ethiopian market but after attending the book club a couple of times I was thinking why not make this app focused on book clubs as well. Therefore, last month I pivoted and made it my mission to customize the app to book clubs. So I added features for creating book clubs, book reading sessions and posting discussion questions. This allows me to be as close as possible to the problem.

# Why build yet another app?

Well I have been building apps for quite some time now and I have more than enough experience as far as technicalities are concerned. But I have never really pushed so much on my own ideas but rather I have always been working for someone. It's not like I haven't tried before but I didn't put enough effort into it in the past. But recently, I have started asking myself why I'm not putting enough effort into my own ideas. Honestly, I have reached a point in my life where working for someone is a little tiring. Therefore, this is my first real attempt to try to make something of my own.

# My approach to building the app

I have worked on an early stage startup before (I was working for someone). Unfortunately, that early stage startup didn't really take off and in the end I got burnt out and decided to quit after two and a half years. This was a while back (I might write something about it) and I have learned a few things about startups and would like to believe I have gained more wisdom. Therefore, I'm doing things slightly differently than before. In the previous startup I mentioned, we failed not because we lacked technical skills or ideas. I believe we failed because of our vision and execution. We tried to be too perfectionist and focused on adding more and more features. But this time, I'm building features that I believe matter. I'm also moving much faster from idea to implementation. The app is already in production with minimal features and I don't spend too much time with UI design. I was literally building the new feature to post discussion questions in the morning before the meetup. I'm not trying to make the app feature packed from the start but rather focus on the things that really matter. Much of my inspiration for this comes from the book titled: [Getting Real](https://basecamp.com/gettingreal).

# Tech details

I've chosen to use Ruby on Rails (RoR), which is literally the OG of web frameworks. It allows me to move faster from idea to implementation and it has everything a web app needs. It's the perfect framework for a solo dev like me and I'm enjoying it so much. I'm also self-hosting the app on my Mac Mini at home. I'm not going to be spending money on a VPS or other hosting service for an idea that might not even work. Thanks to Cloudflare there is no noticeable difference in terms of speed as far as I'm concerned and I don't have that much traffic anyways. It will continue this way and I actually want things to go naturally and slowly.

# A brief summary of "The Stranger"

As I mentioned earlier, this month we read "The Stranger." It was a brief intro about Absurdism. It shows the life of an indifferent man by the name Meursault. From the beginning bro has no feeling or emotion about anything that makes you feel like he's a psychopath. He doesn't feel anything when his own mother dies. At some point he kills a man referred to as the Arab (as the story is set in Algeria) and gets in jail for it. During his trial you get to see the absurdism of the courtroom. Instead of being concerned about the death of a man, the courtroom is concerned about his behavior at his mother's funeral. This is the absurdist part about society that Camus wanted to criticize I suppose.
